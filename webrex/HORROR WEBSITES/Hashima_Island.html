About Hashima Island

"A ghostly reminder of Japan's industrial past." - Hashima Island

Hashima Island, also known as Gunkanjima or Battleship Island, is an abandoned island off the coast of Nagasaki, Japan. Once a thriving coal mining facility, the island is now renowned for its haunting ruins and the remnants of its former life.

In its prime, Hashima was home to thousands of workers and boasted a dense array of concrete buildings. However, as the coal industry declined, the island was abandoned in the 1970s, leaving behind an eerie landscape that tells the story of a once-bustling community.

Today, Hashima Island is a popular tourist destination, attracting those interested in its history and the beauty of its decay. Explore the remnants of the past and experience the unique atmosphere of this ghost town.
