About Project Gutenberg

"There is no friend as loyal as a book." - Ernest Hemingway

Project Gutenberg is a digital library that offers over 60,000 free e-books, primarily from the public domain. Founded in 1971 by Michael S. Hart, it was the first digital library and remains one of the largest resources for free literature online.

Project Gutenberg's collection includes classic literature, historical texts, and a variety of non-fiction works, all freely available in multiple formats for download. The goal is to promote free and easy access to books that have entered the public domain.

Project Gutenberg’s mission is to encourage the creation and distribution of e-books, making literature accessible to readers worldwide.
