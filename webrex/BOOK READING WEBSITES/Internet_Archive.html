About Internet Archive

"Preserving our history is not just an option; it’s a necessity." - Unknown

The Internet Archive is a non-profit digital library offering free access to millions of books, movies, software, music, websites, and more. Founded in 1996, its mission is to provide "universal access to all knowledge."

Key features include the Wayback Machine, which archives the web's history, as well as access to public domain books, digitized films, audio files, and more. The Internet Archive supports researchers, students, and general readers worldwide.

The Internet Archive's mission is to empower universal access to knowledge by preserving and sharing digital content freely for generations to come.
