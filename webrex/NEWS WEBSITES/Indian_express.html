About The Indian Express

"Journalism is a public service." - The Indian Express

The Indian Express is one of India's leading English-language newspapers, known for its commitment to journalism and public service. Founded in 1931, it has built a reputation for investigative reporting, providing in-depth analysis of current events, politics, and social issues.

With a wide range of coverage, The Indian Express aims to inform and engage readers on crucial topics, making it a trusted source for news and analysis in India and beyond. The publication focuses on accuracy, integrity, and accountability in its reporting.

As a pioneer in Indian journalism, The Indian Express continues to strive for excellence, adapting to the changing media landscape while upholding its core values of transparency and truthfulness.
